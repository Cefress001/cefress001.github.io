Care that fits your family's needs.

We refer experienced, independent caregivers for a wide range of in-home care situations, from simple companionship to complex, full-time support.

Every family's situation is unique. We take the time to understand your specific needs so we can refer a caregiver with the right skills, experience, and temperament for your household.

Our Care Services

Companion Care

Social engagement and daily living support for independent seniors

Companion care is designed for individuals who are largely independent but benefit from regular social interaction, light assistance, and a reassuring presence. This service helps reduce isolation, maintain routines, and support overall well-being.

What's Included:

  • Meal Preparation: Planning, cooking, and serving nutritious meals tailored to dietary preferences
  • Light Housekeeping: Tidying living spaces, laundry, and maintaining a clean, comfortable home
  • Transportation & Errands: Doctor appointments, grocery shopping, and social outings
  • Medication Reminders: Timely prompts to ensure medications are taken as prescribed
  • Companionship: Conversation, games, hobbies, and meaningful social engagement

Ideal For:

Seniors who are mobile and cognitively healthy but would benefit from regular companionship, help with household tasks, and support maintaining their independence at home.

Personal Care

Hands-on assistance with activities of daily living (ADLs)

Personal care provides compassionate, professional support with intimate daily activities that many seniors find difficult to manage independently. Our caregivers are trained to provide this assistance with dignity, respect, and discretion.

What's Included:

  • Bathing & Hygiene: Assistance with showers, baths, oral care, and personal grooming
  • Dressing: Help selecting appropriate clothing and getting dressed comfortably
  • Toileting: Respectful support with bathroom needs and incontinence care
  • Mobility Assistance: Help with transfers, walking, and using mobility aids safely
  • Feeding Support: Assistance with eating and drinking for those with limited dexterity

Ideal For:

Individuals with limited mobility, chronic conditions, or physical limitations who need regular hands-on support with personal hygiene and daily activities to maintain comfort and dignity.

Dementia / Memory Care

Specialized support for Alzheimer's and cognitive decline

Memory care requires specialized training, patience, and a deep understanding of cognitive decline. Our caregivers are experienced in managing the unique challenges of dementia, providing structured routines, redirection techniques, and creating safe, calm environments.

What's Included:

  • Cognitive Engagement: Memory games, reminiscence therapy, and activities to stimulate mental function
  • Behavioral Management: Gentle redirection, de-escalation techniques, and managing sundowning
  • Safety Supervision: Preventing wandering, fall prevention, and creating secure environments
  • Routine & Structure: Consistent daily schedules to reduce confusion and anxiety
  • Family Communication: Regular updates on behavior patterns, health changes, and care needs

Ideal For:

Individuals diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease, dementia, or other forms of cognitive impairment who require specialized care, supervision, and a structured environment to ensure safety and quality of life.

Post-Surgery / Post-Hospital Care

Recovery support during critical transition periods

The transition from hospital to home is a vulnerable time. Post-surgical care provides the support needed to ensure a safe, comfortable recovery while reducing the risk of complications or readmission.

What's Included:

  • Transportation Home: Safe transport from hospital or surgical center to home
  • Medication Management: Ensuring prescriptions are filled and taken on schedule
  • Mobility Support: Assistance with walking, transfers, and using assistive devices
  • Wound Care Monitoring: Observing surgical sites and reporting concerns to medical providers
  • Meal Preparation: Nutritious meals to support healing and recovery
  • Light Housekeeping: Maintaining a clean, safe recovery environment

Ideal For:

Individuals recovering from surgery, hospital stays, or medical procedures who need temporary support during the critical recovery period to ensure proper healing and prevent complications.

Overnight & 24/7 Care

Continuous monitoring and support around the clock

Round-the-clock care provides continuous supervision, safety monitoring, and immediate assistance whenever needed. This service ensures that someone is always present to respond to emergencies, provide comfort, and maintain consistent care.

What's Included:

  • Overnight Monitoring: Alert supervision during nighttime hours for safety and assistance
  • 24/7 Availability: Continuous presence with rotating caregivers to prevent fatigue
  • Emergency Response: Immediate assistance with falls, medical concerns, or urgent needs
  • Sleep Support: Help with nighttime toileting, repositioning, and comfort needs
  • Consistent Care: Coordinated team approach to ensure seamless transitions between shifts

Ideal For:

Individuals with advanced care needs, high fall risk, or medical conditions requiring constant supervision. Also ideal for families who need respite from continuous caregiving responsibilities.

Live-In Care

Full-time residential caregiving with continuous presence

Live-in care provides the security of having a dedicated caregiver residing in the home, offering immediate assistance and companionship while allowing your loved one to remain in familiar surroundings. This arrangement requires careful planning and clear expectations.

What's Included:

  • Residential Presence: Caregiver lives in the home with private accommodations
  • Flexible Scheduling: Assistance available throughout the day with scheduled breaks
  • Comprehensive Support: All aspects of companion care, personal care, and household management
  • Household Coordination: Help structuring daily routines, schedules, and responsibilities
  • Compensation Guidance: Clear framework for salary, benefits, and household employer requirements

Ideal For:

Individuals who need consistent presence and support but prefer to remain in their own home. Ideal for those with moderate to high care needs who benefit from the stability of a single, dedicated caregiver.
